Oha Soup

The Description of Oha Soup

Ora (Oha) soup is native to the South Eastern Nigeria. It is a very traditional soup similar to the bitterleaf soup but cooked with Ora leaves. Ora (Oha) Soup is special because the tender ora leaves used in preparing this soup recipe are seasonal unlike their bitterleaf counterpart which can be found all year round.

List of Ingredients for Oha Soup

  • Assorted meats (Beef, goat meat, Ponmo, Shaki are ideal)
  • 2 Cooking Spoon Palm oil
  • 1 Cup Oha leaves (Fresh or Dried)
  • 3-4 Medium Size Cocoyam (Ede)
  • 2 Smoked Fish (Optional)
  • 2-3 Stockfish (Optional)
  • 3 Tablespoons Ground Crayfish
  • 1 Tablespoon Ogiri Okpei
  • 3 Tablespoons Ground Dry pepper or 2 Scotch Bonnet blended
  • 2 Bouillon Cubes
  • Salt to taste

Preparation Steps for Oha Soup

  1. cook the cocoyam to soft
  2. After that, boil the meat
  3. While boiling meat, pound the cocoyam to smooth
  4. prepare the oha leaves
  5. Add Crayfish, smoked Prawns, then add the ground or blended pepper to the meat. Stir and combine
  6. Add Palm oil, leave to thoroughly combine for 3-4 minutes
  7. dd the blended Cocoyam into the stock in small dollops, be careful not to add too much at low heat
  8. Leave to dissolve and combine for 2-3 minutes
  9. then add the Ogiri, stir and combine. Taste for seasoning and adjust if necessary
  10. Then add the prepared Oha Leaves, leave to cook for 2-3 minutes. Do not over cook your vegetables
  11. And it is done
