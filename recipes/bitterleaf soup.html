Bitterleaf Soup

The Description of Bitterleaf Soup

Bitter Leaf Soup, also known as Ofe Onugbu is a popular Nigerian soup. Bitter leaf Soup is peculiar to the Igbo tribe of Eastern Nigeria. A lot of non-Igbos shy away from bitter leaf soup (Well, I did for the longest time) because they think that, due to its name, it is a very bitter soup. But they cannot be more wrong; Ofe Onugbu does not taste bitter in the slightest. The leaves have to be thoroughly washed to get rid of the bitterness before it is added to the soup.

List of Ingredients for Bitterleaf Soup

  • Assorted meats (Beef, goat meat, Ponmo, Shaki are ideal)
  • 2 Cooking Spoon Palm oil
  • 1 Cup Bitter leaves (Fresh or Dried)
  • 3-4 Medium Size Cocoyam (Ede)
  • 2 Smoked Fish (Optional)
  • 2-3 Stockfish (Optional)
  • 3 Tablespoons Ground Crayfish
  • 1 Tablespoon Ogiri Okpei
  • 3 Tablespoons Ground Dry pepper or 2 Scotch Bonnet blended
  • 2 Bouillon Cubes
  • Salt to taste

Preparation Steps for Bitterleaf Soup

  1. cook the cocoyam to soft
  2. After that, boil the meat
  3. While boiling meat, pound the cocoyam to smooth
  4. Wash the bitterleaf with hot water to remove any bitterness
  5. Add Crayfish, smoked Prawns, then add the ground or blended pepper to the meat. Stir and combine
  6. Add Palm oil, leave to thoroughly combine for 3-4 minutes
  7. dd the blended Cocoyam into the stock in small dollops, be careful not to add too much at low heat
  8. Leave to dissolve and combine for 2-3 minutes
  9. hen add the Ogiri, stir and combine. Taste for seasoning and adjust if necessary
  10. Then add the washed Bitter Leaves, leave to cook for 2-3 minutes. Do not over cook your vegetables
  11. And it is done
